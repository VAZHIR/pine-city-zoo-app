GORILLAS

Gorillas are the largest primates and are known for their intelligence and strong social bonds.

They live in groups called troops, led by a dominant silverback

Gorillas are primarily herbivores, feeding on leaves, stems, and fruit.

They are critically endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

Conservation efforts are underway to protect their habitats and reduce poaching.

Gorillas communicate through a variety of vocalizations, body language, and facial expressions.

They are known for their gentle nature, despite their size and strength.

Gorillas play a crucial role in their ecosystems by helping to disperse seeds and maintain forest health.

Observing gorillas in the wild can be a profound experience, offering insights into their complex social structures and behaviors.

WHERE TO FIND THEM

Gorillas are primarily found in the tropical forests of central and western Africa. Some of the best places to observe gorillas in their natural habitat include:

  • Virunga National Park, Democratic Republic of Congo
  • Bwindi Impenetrable National Park, Uganda
  • Volcanoes National Park, Rwanda
  • Kahuzi-Biega National Park, Democratic Republic of Congo
  • Mount Elgon National Park, Uganda
