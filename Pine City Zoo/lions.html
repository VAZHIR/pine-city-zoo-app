LIONS

Lions are known as the "king of the jungle" and are one of the most iconic big cats.

They live in prides, which are social groups consisting of related females, their offspring, and a small number of adult males.

Lions are apex predators, primarily hunting large herbivores such as zebras and wildebeests.

They are known for their powerful roar, which can be heard up to five miles away.

Lions have a unique social structure, with females typically doing most of the hunting while males protect the pride's territory.

Conservation efforts are crucial for lions, as they face threats from habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, and poaching.

Lions are primarily found in sub-Saharan Africa, with a small population in the Gir Forest of India.

WHERE TO FIND THEM

Lions can be found in various regions across Africa, particularly in savannas and grasslands. Some of the best places to observe lions in their natural habitat include:

  • Serengeti National Park, Tanzania
  • Masai Mara National Reserve, Kenya
  • Kruger National Park, South Africa
  • Etosha National Park, Namibia
  • Chobe National Park, Botswana
